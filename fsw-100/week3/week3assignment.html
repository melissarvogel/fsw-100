CIA Realizes It's Been Using Black Highlighters All These Years

Director Demands Investigation

LANGLEY, VA - A report realeased Tuesday by the CIA's Office of the Inspector General revealed that the
CIA has mistakenly obscured hundreds of thousands of pages of critical intelligence information with Black
highlighters.

According to the report, sections of documents - "almost invariably the most crucial passages" - are marred
by an inedible black ink that renders the lines almost impossible to read, t\due to a top-secret highlighting policy that
began at the agency's inseption in 1947.

CIA Director Porter Goss lamented that fact that the public will probably never know the particulars of several
historic events such as:

  • the Cold War
  • the civil-rights movement
  • the growth of the international drug trade
